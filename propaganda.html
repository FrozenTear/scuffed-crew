"But Discord Has E2E Encryption Now"
Yes. Discord's DAVE protocol encrypts voice and video as of March 2026. Credit where it's due — the EFF praised it[4], Trail of Bits audited it, it uses MLS, and the whitepaper is public[5]. We're not going to lie to you and say it doesn't exist.

We're going to explain why it doesn't matter as much as you think.

DAVE Protocol — Autopsy Report

Verdict: Necessary but insufficient
Voice / Video E2E
YES
Real E2E encryption. MLS-based. Audited by Trail of Bits.[5] We acknowledge this is legitimate cryptography. Your voice content is protected from eavesdroppers, including Discord employees. This is good.
Text Messages E2E
NO
Every text message — DMs, private channels, "members only" servers — is stored unencrypted on Discord's servers. Discord explicitly chose not to encrypt text, citing "safety tooling."[6] Your strat discussions, scheduling, and coordination are readable by Discord, by law enforcement with a subpoena, and by anyone who breaches their infrastructure. Which has already happened.
MLS Delivery Service
DISCORD
Discord controls the MLS delivery and authentication service. They facilitate the key exchange. The voice gateway — their server — selects the DAVE protocol version and manages group epoch transitions.[5] The cryptography is real, but the infrastructure running it belongs to a company that also runs an advertising pipeline. You're trusting the locksmith not to keep a copy of the key.
Voice Metadata
EXPOSED
DAVE encrypts the content of your voice. It does not encrypt who is talking, when, for how long, in which channel, with whom, or at what times. Discord's SFU (Selective Forwarding Unit) relays every packet — it knows the shape of every conversation even if it can't hear the words. The NSA's former general counsel said it best: "We kill people based on metadata."
Stage Channels
NO E2E
Stage channels are explicitly excluded from DAVE encryption.[7] If your org uses stages for announcements, town halls, or community events — that's unencrypted. Stream previews are also unencrypted.
Client Control
CLOSED SOURCE
You cannot verify what Discord's client actually does with your data before encryption or after decryption. The DAVE protocol is open. The whitepaper is public. The client that implements it is proprietary. You are trusting closed-source software with your microphone input. Third-party clients are banned by Discord's ToS.[8]
Process Scanning
ACTIVE
While your voice is encrypted, Discord's client continues to scan your running processes for "activity status,"[1] collect system telemetry via the science endpoint, fingerprint your device, and phone home to sentry.io. The mic is encrypted. Everything else about your system is not.
